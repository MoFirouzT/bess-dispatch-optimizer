# Spec R1.2. Degradation cost

**Status:** Approved
**Release:** R1  **Depends on:** R1.1 (deterministic core)

> **Supersedes the 2026-07-11 revision.** This spec replaces the earlier convex-PWL / epigraph degradation model with the **linear DoD-stress** cost now canonical in [formulation.md §R1.2](../formulation.md#r12-degradation-cost). The prior PWL oracles are retired; new linear oracles are below. Implementation (config, code, golden + property tests) is re-derived to match. R1.1 gates are unaffected.

## Objective

Subtract a **linear degradation cost** from the dispatch objective so the optimizer trades arbitrage revenue against cell wear: each MWh of storage-side throughput carries a fixed marginal cost `c_deg` (€/MWh), so the battery cycles only while the price spread clears that cost. This is the *linear power-based* case of the cited cycle-based aging model (Shi 2017 §II-C-1); the cost is native to the LP (no breakpoints, cuts, or special-ordered sets). Degradation is optional; with none configured the model is R1.1 exactly.

## Formulation reference

Implements [formulation.md: "R1.2. Degradation cost"](../formulation.md#r12-degradation-cost) in full. **No math outside that section.** Degradation is a cost subtracted from revenue; it never enters the SoC balance and adds no efficiency term to the objective's cash flow.

## Degradation measure (summary)

Per-period **storage-side throughput**, counting **both directions**, and its linear cost:

```text
τ_t = η_ch · p_charge_t · dt  +  (p_discharge_t / η_dis) · dt
D_t = c_deg · τ_t
```

`c_deg` (€/MWh) is a single marginal wear cost. Efficiency appears in `τ_t` because it is cell-side energy (a wear quantity), not cash flow, so a round trip of depth `q` is penalized on both its charge and its discharge period. Because `Σ_t D_t = c_deg · Σ_t τ_t` is the total energy through the cell times a constant, the horizon cost is independent of the time step `dt`: the property the linear form restores (the retired PWL basis was per-unit of `τ_max`, coupling it indirectly to `dt`). No `τ_max` is needed.

## Parameters / configuration

`DegradationSpec` collapses to a single field, optionally attached to `BatterySpec` (default `None` ⇒ R1.1 behavior):

| Field | Meaning | Unit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `cost_per_mwh` | marginal wear cost `c_deg` per unit storage-side throughput; `≥ 0` | €/MWh |

Validation (Pydantic v2): `cost_per_mwh ≥ 0`. Grounded values sit near €7–15/MWh (cell replacement cost ÷ lifetime throughput; formulation §R1.2). `c_deg = 0` reduces to R1.1 (same result as `None`, but keeps the term present).

## Golden oracles (exact expected values)

1 MWh / 1 MW battery, `dt=1`, `e_0=e_tgt=0`. Tolerance `1e-6`. A **linear** cost makes the cycle **bang-bang**: with a constant per-MWh cost the trade is all-or-nothing, unlike the retired PWL model's partial `q*`.

| # | inputs | expected objective | expected schedule | why this case |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | T=2, π=[0,50], η=1, c_deg=10 | **30.0** | charge [1,0]; discharge [0,1]; soc [1,0] | cheap wear: round-trip cost 2·c_deg=20 < spread 50 ⇒ full cycle; obj = 50 − 2·c_deg |
| 2 | T=2, π=[0,50], η=1, c_deg=30 | **0.0** | idle; soc [0,0] | expensive wear: 2·c_deg=60 > spread 50 ⇒ don't trade; breakeven at c_deg=25 |
| 3 | T=2, π=[0,50], η_ch=1, η_dis=0.8, c_deg=10 | **20.0** | charge [1,0]; discharge [0,0.8]; soc [1,0] | storage-side: τ is cell-side (discharge τ = p_dis/η_dis = 1, not 0.8); a grid-side cost gives 22, not 20 |
| 4 | T=3, π=[10,50,20], degradation=None | **40.0** | charge [1,0,0]; discharge [0,1,0]; soc [1,0,0] | regression: disabled ⇒ exactly R1.1 oracle 1 |

**Oracles 1 & 2 (bang-bang).** Terminal 0 ⇒ discharge = charge = `q`; with `η=1`, `τ=q` per period, so `f(q) = 50q − 2·c_deg·q = (50 − 2·c_deg)·q`, linear in `q ∈ [0,1]`. Slope > 0 (`c_deg < 25`) ⇒ `q*=1`; slope < 0 (`c_deg > 25`) ⇒ `q*=0`. At `c_deg=10`, obj = 30; at `c_deg=30`, obj = 0; breakeven `c_deg = 25`. (Matches the formulation §R1.2 worked example.)

**Oracle 3 (storage-side).** Charge `q` at price 0: `soc_0 = η_ch·q = q`, charge-side `τ_0 = η_ch·q = q`. Discharge to terminal 0: `p_dis = η_dis·soc_0 = 0.8q`, discharge-side `τ_1 = p_dis/η_dis = q`. Revenue `50·0.8q = 40q`; degradation `c_deg·(τ_0+τ_1) = 2·c_deg·q`. So `f(q) = (40 − 2·c_deg)·q`; at `c_deg=10` the slope is 20 > 0 ⇒ `q*=1`, `p_dis=0.8`, obj **20**. A grid-side implementation scores discharge throughput as `p_dis=0.8` (not `1`), giving `f=(40 − 1.8·c_deg)q ⇒ obj 22`: the number this oracle pins.

## Property tests (invariants for any valid input)

- **All R1.1 invariants still hold** (SoC bounds, power caps, mutual exclusion, exact SoC continuity, terminal, objective floor); degradation touches none of them.
- **Δt-invariance (the headline linear property):** for a fixed physical dispatch, total degradation `Σ_t D_t` is equal at `dt=1` and `dt=0.25`, because it is `c_deg × total cell throughput`, independent of the time partition.
- **Objective consistency:** solver objective ≈ `Σ_t π_t·dt·(p_dis − p_ch) − c_deg·Σ_t τ_t`, with `τ_t = η_ch·p_ch·dt + p_dis/η_dis·dt`.
- **Monotone:** `c_deg ≥ 0` ⇒ more throughput never lowers cost, and a higher `c_deg` never raises the objective.
- **Degradation never pays:** objective with degradation ≤ objective without (same prices) `+ ε`.
- **Reduces to R1.1:** at `c_deg = 0` and at `degradation=None`, objective and schedule match the R1.1 solve within tol.
- **Scale invariance:** `c_deg` is €/MWh, so scaling the asset's power and energy ratings by the same factor scales revenue and throughput together and leaves the *marginal* wear cost unchanged (the "spec-invariant" property, formulation §R1.2).

## Out of scope (explicit; do not build here)

- **Nonlinear convex DoD stress** (`Φ(d)=k₂·d·e^{k₃d}` or `k₄·d^{k₅}`; Xu 2018): the *more accurate* model, capturing the deep-cycle penalty (an NMC cell loses roughly 10× more life at 100% vs 10% DoD). Convex in the SoC profile (Shi 2017 Thm 1) but needs rainflow cycle identification (no closed form) ⇒ convex-but-not-LP; solvable by subgradient or a cycle-detection MILP with a convex-PWL epigraph of `Φ`. Referenced future work (formulation §R1.2).
- **Convex-PWL cost on per-period throughput (the retired model).** Do not re-add. It penalizes instantaneous power rather than cycle depth, so it matched no published source; the linear throughput cost is the grounded throughput-based form.
- **Rainflow** cycle counting and **calendar aging** (time-based capacity fade): harder / different in kind (rainflow is path-dependent, not a per-period cost). Deferred, not faked.
- **EFC (equivalent-full-cycle) normalization** and **direction-specific wear curves** (charge vs discharge aging differently): reparametrizations / refinements that only earn their place against real cycle-life data; R1.2 uses one shared throughput cost.
- Validation (R1.3), backtest / real data (R1.4), API (R1.5), all of R2.
