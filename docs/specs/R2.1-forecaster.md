# Spec R2.1. Probabilistic price forecaster (conformal intervals)

**Status:** Implemented (gate green; five resolved decisions below)
**Release:** R2  **Depends on:** R1.4 (walk-forward + leakage discipline), R1.4b (ENTSO-E loader), R1.4c (ingestion guard, the forecaster consumes guarded prices)

## Objective

A probabilistic day-ahead price forecaster in `bess.forecaster` that outputs calibrated **prediction intervals**, not point estimates: a gradient-boosted model (LightGBM) wrapped in **split/conformalized-quantile prediction** (via MAPIE) so the intervals carry a distribution-free coverage guarantee, validated by empirical coverage matching the nominal level under walk-forward. This is the first Release-2 module and the input the stochastic layer (R2.2+) samples scenarios from; forecast *uncertainty*, not a single number.

## Formulation reference

**No optimizer delta.** R2.1 adds no constraint, objective term, or efficiency placement to the MILP; `docs/formulation.md`'s dispatch math is unchanged. The one new *mathematical* claim R2.1 introduces is the **conformal coverage guarantee**, which (per `CLAUDE.md` §1's one-section-per-part pattern) should get a brief, self-contained `formulation.md` §R2.1 summary, the split-conformal / CQR construction and its marginal-coverage property, pinned to the governing reference. **Open question (below):** confirm the coverage summary belongs in `formulation.md` versus staying in this spec + `references.md`. No `formulation.md` text is written until that and this spec are approved.

## Governing reference (to record in `docs/references.md`)

- **Governing (the new theory; conformal prediction):** A. Angelopoulos & S. Bates, *A Gentle Introduction to Conformal Prediction and Distribution-Free Uncertainty Quantification* (tutorial). Authority for split/inductive conformal, distribution-free **marginal** coverage under exchangeability, and conformalized quantile regression (CQR). Edition/section pinned and **verified before relying**.
- **Secondary (forecasting methodology, pointer only):** Hyndman & Athanasopoulos, *Forecasting: Principles and Practice* (OTexts, free); feature/calendar construction and honest time-series evaluation.
- **Secondary (gradient boosting, pointer only):** Hastie, Tibshirani & Friedman, *Elements of Statistical Learning* (free), the boosted-tree learner.
- **Notation reconciliation:** house style wins. Prices stay `π_t` (€/MWh, grid-side) on the UTC series schema (conventions §1/§4); the target is the day-ahead price at a future timestamp, features are strictly-past-derived. Conformal's `α` (miscoverage) maps to `confidence_level = 1 − α`.
- **Alternatives considered:** *bare quantile regression* (LightGBM quantile objective alone) gives quantiles but **no finite-sample coverage guarantee**; kept as the CQR base learner, not the deliverable. *Bootstrap / Gaussian residual intervals* are heavier and distribution-assuming; rejected in favour of distribution-free conformal.

## Design decisions (to be recorded as ADRs)

- **ADR-0014. CQR (adaptive width) over split conformal (constant width).** Day-ahead prices are heteroscedastic (evening-peak and scarcity hours are far more volatile than night), so a **constant-width** interval is miscalibrated *conditionally* even when marginally correct. Conformalized quantile regression on LightGBM quantile models gives **hour-adaptive** interval width. Trade-off: three base models (lower/upper/point) instead of one, and CQR's coverage is still only marginal. Split conformal is kept as the simpler baseline the coverage gate compares against.
- **Features are strictly lagged (no leakage by construction)**: a design principle, not a separate ADR (it applies the R1.4 leakage discipline). Every feature at target time `t` is a function of prices/calendar available **before gate closure** for `t`; no same-day or future price enters. Purging/embargo now matter because a model is fit.

## Scope decisions

- **Features v1: lagged prices + calendar only.** Lags (e.g. `t−24h, t−48h, t−168h`), rolling stats, and calendar (hour, weekday, month, holiday). These are **derivable from the price series itself**: no new data source, so R2.1 stays self-contained and token-free-testable.
- **Weather features are deferred** (see Open questions). Temp/wind genuinely help, but they are a *whole new data-acquisition path* (loader, source, licensing) mirroring R1.4b. Adding them now would couple the conformal-coverage gate to an external feed. Recommendation: ship R2.1 on price+calendar features, add weather as R2.1c or named future work.

MAPIE **1.x** API (verified end-to-end this session against `mapie==1.4.1` / `lightgbm==4.6.0`):

- **Split conformal:** `SplitConformalRegressor(point_estimator, confidence_level=0.9, prefit=False).fit(X_tr, y_tr).conformalize(X_cal, y_cal)`, then `predict_interval(X) → (pred, interval)` with `interval[:, 0, 0]` = lower, `interval[:, 1, 0]` = upper. Toy coverage 0.95.
- **CQR:** MAPIE 1.x will **not** accept a single LightGBM (it can't set the quantile param); pass **three prefit LGBM quantile models in order `[lower(α/2), upper(1−α/2), median]`** to `ConformalizedQuantileRegressor(models, confidence_level=0.9, prefit=True).conformalize(X_cal, y_cal)`. Toy coverage 0.91. This 3-prefit-model pattern is the implementation contract.

MAPIE emits a benign `X does not have valid feature names` warning when fed numpy after fitting on a named frame; feed consistent types (numpy or frame) throughout. Pin the MAPIE major version (API changed at 1.0).

## Gates (statistical, not a hand-solved oracle)

Forecasting has no exact golden oracle; be honest that these are **statistical** gates (like R1.4c's classifier, not the MILP's un-fakeable arithmetic). The un-fakeable anchors here are *coverage on data the model did not calibrate on* and *deterministic reproducibility*.

- **Coverage gate (headline):** under walk-forward on the synthetic series, empirical coverage of `[lower, upper]` at nominal `0.9` lands within a stated tolerance band (e.g. `0.9 ± 0.05`, tolerance justified by calibration-set size, not tuned to pass). Re-validated on real ENTSO-E in the integration test.
- **Reproducibility gate (golden-analog):** fixed seed + fixed inputs → bit-stable point/interval outputs, so the pipeline is regression-testable.

## Property tests (invariants for any generated input)

- **Interval ordering:** `lower ≤ point ≤ upper` at every target, always.
- **Monotone in confidence:** a higher `confidence_level` yields intervals no narrower than a lower one (nested coverage).
- **No leakage:** features built at `t` are invariant to any mutation of `π_{t'}` for `t'` at/after gate closure for `t` (the un-fakeable leakage check).
- **Coverage on exchangeable synthetic data** ≥ nominal − tolerance across Hypothesis-seeded noise draws.

## Out of scope (explicit; do not build here)

- **Weather / exogenous features**: deferred (data-acquisition path + licensing); R2.1 is price+calendar.
- **Scenario generation / reduction**: R2.2 samples from this forecaster's intervals.
- **Stochastic / recourse optimization**: R2.3; R2.1 only produces the uncertainty input.
- **The drift monitor**: R2.1b (a separate signal: model decay, not interval calibration).
- **Multi-zone / cross-border joint forecasting, hierarchical reconciliation**: single zone at a time.
- **Deep-learning forecasters (N-BEATS, TFT)**: a boosted-tree baseline is the scope; conformal is the differentiator, not the base learner.

## Resolved decisions (reviewed 2026-07-01)

1. **Method**: **resolved: CQR default** (adaptive width), split conformal kept as the compared baseline (ADR-0014). Justified by day-ahead heteroscedasticity.
2. **Weather**: **resolved: deferred.** R2.1 ships on lagged-price + calendar features (self-contained, token-free-testable). Weather is its own R1.4b-shaped loader + licensing path → R2.1c / named future work.
3. **Conformal summary in `formulation.md`**: **resolved: yes, a brief §R2.1**, following the one-section-per-part pattern (§1). It summarizes the split/CQR coverage guarantee only (no optimizer constraint), pinned to Angelopoulos & Bates.
4. **Dependency weight**: **resolved: behind an optional `forecast` extra** (`lightgbm`, `mapie`, `scikit-learn`, `holidays`), mirroring the `examples` matplotlib group. The core install and the R1 CI job stay lean; a dedicated CI step installs the extra and runs the coverage gate.
5. **Coverage tolerance**: **resolved: `0.9 ± 0.05`** (nominal 90%, empirical ∈ [0.85, 0.95]), justified by the walk-forward calibration-set size, not tuned to pass. Split conformal's finite-sample guarantee makes ≈ nominal coverage the expected outcome when the wrapper is correct.
