# Spec R2.2c. Residual-load-conditional scenario tail

**Status:** Implemented (gate green; channel = magnitude, confirmed by the human 2026-07-24; four resolved decisions below, [ADR-0026](../decisions/0026-conditional-scale-tail-channel.md))
**Release:** R2  **Depends on:** R2.2b (the unconditional GPD tail this conditions), R2.1c (supplies the residual-load covariate, the whole reason this is possible), R2.2 (bootstrap), R2.1 (residual history)

## Objective

Make the R2.2b extreme-value tail **conditional on the target day's per-hour forecast residual load**, so spikes concentrate where they physically occur: tight-margin hours (high load, low renewables). R2.2b applies one unconditional GPD to every hour of every scenario; R2.2c ties the tail to the covariate R2.1c added, so the R2.3 program reserves SoC on the hours that actually risk a spike, not uniformly.

## Motivation (why this is the point of the whole thread)

The forecasting-challenges audit's core insight was that a day-ahead **price spike is a residual-load-extreme event** (scarcity when demand is high and must-run renewables are low). R2.1c put residual load into the forecaster; R2.2b un-capped the spike magnitude but spread it **uniformly** across all hours, blind to conditions. So a windy, slack hour gets the same spike tail as a tight evening peak. That is exactly the blindness R2.1c was meant to remove, one layer down. R2.2c closes it: the tail is heavier (and/or more frequent) on high-residual-load hours, so the scenario set predicts spikes *when* they happen, and the CVaR reservation follows.

## The design fork (resolve before implementing)

Two conditioning channels, both tie the tail to residual load `x`; they differ in what depends on `x`:

- **Magnitude channel (conditional GPD scale `β(x)`).** Keep R2.2b's splice (which hours exceed comes from the bootstrap), but scale the spike *size* by residual load: `β(x) = β₀·exp(γ·z(x))`, `z` standardized residual load, `γ ≥ 0`. A one-function change to `apply_tail`; clean `γ=0 ⇒ exactly R2.2b` identity; `γ` fit by OLS of log-excess on `z` among exceedances (pure-numpy, no MLE/scipy). Spikes are *bigger* on tight hours.
- **Frequency channel (conditional exceedance rate `λ(x)`).** Model *which* hours spike as a function of residual load (spikes concentrate on tight hours), magnitude from R2.2b's unconditional GPD. More decision-relevant (it predicts spike *timing*, which drives SoC reservation) but a larger redesign: it decouples spike location from the bootstrap and needs a rate model (`λ` a monotone binned/pooled estimate of `P(exceed | x)`), and it is more data-hungry.

**Proposed (subject to your call): the magnitude channel for R2.2c**, the clean, testable, pure-numpy extension of R2.2b with the exact identity, with the frequency channel deferred to R2.2d (named, on record), because it is the harder redesign and both channels shift the CVaR reservation toward tight hours. If you would rather R2.2c *be* the frequency channel (the more faithful "spikes on the right hours"), say so and the spec below is rewritten around `λ(x)`.

## Governing reference

- **Governing:** S. Coles, *An Introduction to Statistical Modeling of Extreme Values* (Springer, 2001), **ch. 6 "Extremes of Nonstationary Sequences"**, where GPD/GEV parameters are made covariate-dependent through a link function (e.g. a log-link on the scale). The R2.2b threshold-model chapter (ch. 4) still governs the base tail. Chapter title/number verified against the publisher listing before relying.
- **Notation reconciliation:** house style wins. Covariate `x` is the R2.1c residual load `load_da − wind_da − solar_da` (MW, per target hour), standardized to `z`; the GPD scale link is `β(x) = β₀·exp(γ z)`, shape `ξ` reused from R2.2b (unconditional; conditioning `ξ` is out of scope, see below).
- **Alternatives considered:** a *fully covariate-dependent MLE GPD* (both `ξ(x)` and `β(x)` by numerical MLE) is the textbook non-stationary fit but reintroduces an optimizer/scipy and is data-hungry for `ξ(x)`; rejected for the log-link scale-only fit. *Regime-split GPD* (separate fit per residual-load tercile) wastes the pooled exceedances and thins each bin; rejected in favour of the pooled regression.

## Design sketch (magnitude channel; subject to the fork above)

- **Fit `γ` by OLS, pure-numpy.** For a GPD, `E[excess] = β/(1−ξ)`, so with `ξ` fixed (the R2.2b PWM fit) and `β(x) = β₀ exp(γ z)`, the mean log-excess is affine in `z` with slope `γ`. Regress `log(excess)` on `z` among the exceedances → `γ̂` (OLS slope), `β̂₀` from the intercept and the closed-form `E[log excess]` offset. Constrain `γ ≥ 0` (a spike tail should not get *lighter* on tighter hours); clamp negative fits to 0 and record it.
- **Generation.** `apply_tail` takes the per-hour target covariate `z_target` and, at each spliced exceedance in hour `t`, draws the excess from `GPD(ξ, β(z_target[t]))`. `γ=0` (or no covariate) is byte-identical to R2.2b.

## Golden oracles

| # | inputs | expected | why |
|---|--------|----------|-----|
| 1 | `γ = 0` (or no covariate) | scenario set byte-identical to R2.2b | opt-in identity: conditioning off ⇒ exactly R2.2b |
| 2 | fixed (excess, `z`) sample | OLS `γ̂`, `β̂₀` equal a hand-computed value | pins the conditional fit arithmetic |
| 3 | two hours, `z_hi > z_lo`, fixed seed | the high-`z` hour's spliced excess uses the larger `β` (heavier) | pins the per-hour conditioning |
| 4 | a fit that would give `γ < 0` | `γ` clamped to 0, flagged | pins the monotonicity guard |

## Property tests

- **Opt-in identity:** `γ=0` / conditional tail with a constant covariate ⇒ bit-identical to R2.2b.
- **Monotone in the covariate:** higher residual load ⇒ stochastically heavier spike (larger expected excess), over fixed seeds.
- **`γ ≥ 0` guard:** the fit never returns a negative slope (clamped).
- **Body preserved / valid measure / determinism:** as R2.2b.
- **Reduces to R2.2b in distribution** when the covariate carries no signal (random `z`): `γ̂ ≈ 0`.

## Acceptance gate

*Blocks:* nothing downstream is hard (R2.3 consumes the set). Proves the conditioning *earns its place*. Every box passes.

- [x] **Conditional signal (headline, honest):** on real ENTSO-E NL (Feb–Jun 2024, verified live 2026-07-24), the fitted **`γ = 0.21 > 0`**: residual load genuinely predicts spike magnitude, so the tail scale rises from **`β ≈ 6.2` on slack hours (10th-pct residual load) to `β ≈ 10.4` on tight hours (90th-pct), about +69%**. The conditioning reaches generation (a high-residual-load target hour draws larger spikes than a low one). Reported with provenance; a future asset/window with no signal fits `γ ≈ 0` and reduces to R2.2b, a reported null (the R2.5 / R2.1c honesty rule).
- [x] **Fit / conditioning goldens** pass exactly (OLS slope exact; `β₀` = R2.2b PWM scale; per-hour β conditioning; `γ<0` clamp); **opt-in identity + monotonicity + no-signal** properties pass.

## Out of scope

- **Frequency channel `λ(x)`** (conditional exceedance *rate*): the more decision-relevant but larger redesign, deferred to R2.2d unless the fork above flips it into R2.2c.
- **Conditioning the shape `ξ(x)`:** data-hungry and unstable; `ξ` stays the unconditional R2.2b fit.
- **Full covariate-MLE GPD, regime-split fits, multivariate/extremal-dependence tails.**
- **Any optimizer / R2.3 change.**
